Title: The Mountain Hike
Level: Advanced

The trail to Eagle Point wound steeply upward through a forest of tall
pines, and by the time Noor reached the halfway marker, her legs had
begun to ache in a way that felt almost satisfying. Her father walked
a few paces ahead, pausing occasionally to point out a woodpecker or an
unusual cluster of mushrooms growing from a fallen log.

"We don't have to reach the top," he reminded her, noticing how she
pressed a hand to her side. "The view from here is already pretty
incredible."

Noor considered this. Part of her wanted to stop, to sit on a sun-warmed
rock and simply breathe in the smell of pine needles. But another part
of her remembered why she had asked to come on this hike in the first
place — she wanted to see the valley from the very top, the way her
father always described it.

She adjusted the straps of her backpack and kept walking. An hour later,
when the trees finally opened onto a wide granite ledge, the valley
spread out below them in a patchwork of green fields and a winding
silver river. Noor forgot all about her tired legs. Some views, she
decided, were worth every aching step.

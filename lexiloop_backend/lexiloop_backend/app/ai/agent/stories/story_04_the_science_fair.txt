Title: The Science Fair Surprise
Level: Intermediate

Every year, Lincoln Elementary held a science fair in the gymnasium.
Diego had signed up weeks ago, but he still hadn't picked a project.
His friend Maya suggested growing plants under different colors of
light.

Diego set up three small pots of bean seeds. One sat under a red lamp,
one under a blue lamp, and one under normal sunlight. Every morning
before school, he measured how tall each plant had grown and wrote the
numbers in a notebook.

After two weeks, something surprising happened. The plant under the
blue light grew taller and greener than the other two. Diego hadn't
expected that at all — he had guessed the sunlight plant would win
easily.

At the fair, Diego explained his results to the judges, pointing to his
neat chart of measurements. They were impressed that his data told a
story he hadn't predicted. Diego learned that the best experiments are
sometimes the ones that surprise you.

Title: The Time Capsule
Level: Advanced

When Ms. Alvarez announced that the fifth-grade class would be burying a
time capsule to be opened in twenty-five years, the classroom erupted
into excited chatter. Everyone had opinions about what should go inside,
and for once, even the quietest students had something to say.

Leo suggested a photograph of the whole class, arguing that no one would
remember what they looked like as children by the time the capsule was
opened. Priya wanted to include a letter describing what life was like
in their town, in case things changed too much to recognize. Someone
else insisted on adding a list of everyone's favorite songs, which led
to a lengthy and good-natured debate about musical taste.

In the end, the class compiled all of their ideas into a single metal
box: photographs, letters, a folded newspaper, and a small jar of soil
from the school garden. As they lowered the box into the ground beneath
the oak tree by the playground, Leo wondered what kind of person he
would be when it was finally dug up again. Twenty-five years felt both
impossibly far away and, strangely, not far away at all.
